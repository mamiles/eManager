Manual Organization

The first group of chapters introduce the basics of Rendezvous software:

  • Chapter 1, Product Overview is a high-level introduction to Rendezvous software.
  • Chapter 2, Architecture introduces architecture concepts, and explains specific architectural elements.
  • Chapter 3, Fundamentals discusses the details of messages and subject-based addressing.™
  • Chapter 4, The Rendezvous Daemon introduces rvd—the daemon process.
  • Chapter 5, Subject Names presents details of subject name syntax and semantics.

The second group of chapters discuss important concepts of the Rendezvous programming architecture:

  • Chapter 6, Data presents details of datatypes, formats and storage issues.
  • Chapter 7, Events presents events, queues, dispatch, callback functions, and event semantics.
  • Chapter 8, Transport presents transports and their parameters.
  • Chapter 9, Virtual Circuits presents virtual circuits.
  • Chapter 10, Guidelines for Programming is a collection of rules of thumb for Rendezvous programmers.

The third group of chapters present quality of service features:

  • Chapter 11, Certified Message Delivery
  • Chapter 12, Distributed Queue
  • Chapter 13, Fault Tolerance Concepts
  • Chapter 14, Fault Tolerance Programming
  • Chapter 15, Developing Fault-Tolerant Programs
